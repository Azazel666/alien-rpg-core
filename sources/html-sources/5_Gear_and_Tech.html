Weapons

YOU ALWAYS WANT a weapon close at hand – you never know where danger lurks. Don’t expect to have your choice of firearms, however. When something hisses at you in the dark, you can’t be too choosy, understand? You use whatever you can get your hands on to buy you some time. The tables and images on the following pages show various kinds of common weapons.

Weapon Features

Different weapons neutralize different threats. This list is a heads-up on the features used in the weapon tables.

  • Modifier:

    The modifier to CLOSE COMBAT or RANGED COMBAT when using the weapon.

  • Damage:

    The amount of damage inflicted on a successful attack. Extra rolled beyond the first increase the damage. The letter E by the number means that the attack is explosive (page 76). "Stun" means that the weapon inflicts stun damage (page 71) instead of regular damage.

  • Range:

    The minimum / maximum range. Any attack closer than the minimum range gets –2 dice per range band below the minimum range. A weapon cannot be used beyond its maximum range.

  • Ammo:

    The ammo supply rating of the weapon (page 29). When the ammo supply rating runs out, you need to reload. "Single-shot" means the weapon needs to be reloaded (quick action) after each attack.

  • Power:

    The power supply rating of the weapon (page 29), if it has one. When the power supply runs out, the weapons needs to be recharged or reloaded.

  • Weight:

    The number of rows the weapon requires in the inventory list. If the weight is indicated as N/A, the weapon is too heavy to be carried.

  • Cost:

    The typical price in W-Y dollars. The actual price varies depending on the local supply and demand.

  • Special:

    Any special effects of the weapon.

    Armor piercing means that all armor levels are reduced 1 step against this weapon to a minimum of zero).

    Full auto means the weapon is capable of fully automatic fire (page 67).

    Unreliable means that a Mess Up (7+) result on a stress response roll causes a jam (in addition to other effects). Clearing a jam is a full action and requires a HEAVY MACHINERY roll.

Ammo

One full reload for a firearm typically costs about 5% of the cost of the weapon itself. A reload typically has a weight of ¼. The ammo inside a loaded weapon is included in the weapon’s weight.

Dirty Bullets

Here’s where things start getting particularly nasty. The target of these radioactive bullets not only suffers piercing damage, but their wounds are irradiated in the process. Any ammunition type can be treated with radionuclides, but it’s going to run you four times the cost of normal ammo.

Make sure you take the proper precautionary steps when using them—dirty bullets not only leave your gun glowing in the dark, but also your shooting hand and holster hip riddled with cancer. Anyone hit by a dirty bullet suffers 1 rad (page 77) in addition to normal damage. Carrying a weapon loaded with dirty bullets gives you 1 rad per shift.

Data Storage

ALONG WITH A GUM WRAPPER and a few dirty tissues, people always need to carry information in their pocket. In the 22nd century, that is usually accomplished in the following ways.

Long-Data Discs

  • Weight –
  • Cost $2,000
  • Effect Holds up to 10 zetta￾bytes (ZB) of data.

After several data losses from electromagnetic pulses in the first half of the 21st century, the move was made to reintroduce and reinforce physical data storage. Weyland Corp scientists took optical disc media and enhanced it to the next level. The result is a nano-optical long-data memory disc. All colony and corporate records are backed up on these discs in case of an electromagnetic discharge.

Magnetic Tape

  • Weight –
  • Cost $5
  • Effect Holds up to 120 tera￾bytes (TB) of data.

A two-hundred-year-old technology, magnetic tape cassettes have become popular on the Frontier simply because they are both disposable and cheap. Utilizing sputter deposition technology, these cassettes can hold between 60, 90, or 120 terabytes (TB) of information each.

While easily disrupted by a strong magnetic pulse, the benefit of magnetic tapes is that the archaic technology that runs them produces no easily detected wave signals. Most security sensor grids are set to detect more sophisticated electronic hardware. All these factors combined have managed to keep magnetic tapes in vogue for the past fifty years.

Vehicle Features

This list is a heads-up on the features used in the vehicle listings.

  • Encountered: Where the vehicle is likely to be encountered.
  • Length: The length of the vehicle, in meters.
  • Passengers: The number of people that can ride in the vehicle, excluding the driver.
  • Maneuverability: A modifier to your PILOTING rolls when performing challenging maneuvers.
  • Speed: The speed of the vehicle, in number of zones per driving action.
  • Hull: How much damage the vehicle can take before becoming wrecked.
  • Armor: The armor level of the vehicle.
  • Weapons: The typical armaments mounted on the vehicle, if any. See the list on page 313 for stats.
  • Cost: The typical price in W-Y dollars. The actual price varies depending on the local supply and demand.
